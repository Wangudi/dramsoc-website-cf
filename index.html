About Us

Who We Are

The Strathmore Drama Society (DRAMSOC) is a vibrant student-led organization at Strathmore University, dedicated to nurturing artistic talent, fostering creativity, and driving social impact through theatre and film. Established in 2006, DRAMSOC has grown into a leading platform for students to explore, develop, and showcase their theatrical and cinematic talents. Since its inception, it has trained and nurtured hundreds of students in acting, directing, scriptwriting, and technical theatre. We have driven social impact through drama and expanded its reach beyond Strathmore University, engaging communities across Kenya through outreach programs and performances.

Our Mission

To provide a dynamic platform for students and the community to explore, develop, and showcase their theatrical and cinematic talents while using drama as a tool for education, social change, and entertainment.

Our Vision

To be a premier arts institution in Kenya and beyond, inspiring creativity, fostering cultural dialogue, and empowering individuals to use the transformative power of theatre and film for personal and societal growth.

Our Motto

"Take It to the Next Level!"

Our Core Values

  • Quality: Excellence in all productions and programs.
  • Integrity: Transparency and accountability in all operations.
  • Creativity: Innovation in storytelling and artistic expression.
  • Honesty: Authenticity in all interactions and engagements.

What We Do

  • Productions: Annual theatrical plays and short films that address social issues and entertain audiences.
  • Training: Workshops and mentorship programs in acting, directing, cinematography, and more.
  • Outreach: Community programs that use drama to educate and inspire underserved populations.
  • Events: Open mic nights, and collaborative projects with other clubs and organizations.

Our Impact

Over the years, DRAMSOC has:

  • Empowered young talent, with many alumni pursuing careers in theatre, film, and media.
  • Addressed critical social issues such as mental health, gender equality, and environmental conservation through impactful productions.
  • Built a strong alumni network that continues to support and mentor current members.

Production Team

Every production features a dedicated creative and technical team assembled specifically for that show. Our production teams typically include:

  • Director - The artistic visionary who interprets the script, guides actors, coordinates all creative elements, and shapes the overall production. Directors work closely with all departments to realize their creative vision.
  • Stage Manager - The organizational backbone of any production, managing rehearsals, calling cues during performances, coordinating backstage operations, and ensuring everything runs smoothly from tech week through closing night.
  • Cast - Our talented actors who breathe life into characters, undergo rigorous rehearsals, develop chemistry as an ensemble, and deliver powerful performances that captivate audiences night after night.
  • Technical Crew - The skilled technicians managing lighting, sound, set construction, props, and special effects. They create the physical and atmospheric world that supports the storytelling and enhances the audience experience.
  • Costume & Makeup Team - Artists who design and create costumes, apply makeup, manage wardrobe, and handle quick changes. They transform actors into characters through visual storytelling and period-appropriate styling.
  • PR & Marketing Team - The publicity force behind every show, creating promotional materials, managing social media campaigns, engaging audiences, documenting the production process, and ensuring strong ticket sales.

Production teams are announced for each show. Check our social media and articles section for updates on upcoming productions and their creative teams!

Our Departments

The Backbone of DRAMSOC Excellence

DRAMSOC operates through specialized departments, each contributing unique expertise to create theatrical magic. Our department structure ensures that every production receives professional attention in all aspects - from concept to curtain call.

  • Overall artistic vision and direction for productions
  • Script selection and interpretation
  • Casting actors and assembling creative teams
  • Blocking, staging, and choreography
  • Rehearsal scheduling and management
  • Coordinating all departments

Sample Activities: Directors' workshops, script analysis sessions, blocking rehearsals, character development exercises, production meetings, tech rehearsals, dress rehearsals.

Skills Developed: Leadership, creative vision, communication, collaboration, time management, artistic interpretation, decision-making under pressure.

This department shapes the artistic heart of every production, transforming words on a page into living, breathing theatre.

How Departments Work Together

While each department has specialized responsibilities, the magic of theatre happens when all departments collaborate seamlessly. A typical production involves:

  • Pre-Production: Directing & Production leads script selection, with input from Scriptwriting & Research. Finance & Sponsorship develops the budget while PR & Marketing plans the publicity campaign. Technical begins design concepts and Costume & Makeup starts research.
  • Rehearsal Period: Directing & Production works with actors while Training & Development provides supplementary skills workshops. Technical builds sets and programs lighting. Costume & Makeup creates and fits costumes. PR & Marketing shoots promotional content.
  • Tech Week: All departments intensify collaboration. Technical integrates all technical elements. Costume & Makeup finalizes all looks and organizes quick changes. Directing & Production coordinates tech and dress rehearsals. PR & Marketing amplifies publicity.
  • Performance Run: All departments support live performances, troubleshoot issues, and ensure smooth operations night after night.
  • Post-Production: Departments document the production, evaluate successes and challenges, and begin planning for the next show.

"Theatre is the ultimate collaborative art form. In DRAMSOC, we don't just make theatre - we build family."

The skills developed through drama - confidence, communication, collaboration, creativity, and critical thinking - translate powerfully to any career path.

Notable Alumni Success Stories

Film & Television

Several of our alumni have pursued successful careers in the film and television industry, working as actors, directors, producers, cinematographers, and screenwriters. They've appeared in local and international productions, bringing the storytelling skills honed at DRAMSOC to screens around the world.

Theatre & Performing Arts

Many DRAMSOC members have continued in professional theatre, working with major theatre companies, directing acclaimed productions, teaching drama, and establishing their own theatre groups. They carry forward the spirit of excellence and innovation that defines DRAMSOC.

Media & Communications

DRAMSOC's emphasis on communication and public performance has equipped alumni for success in journalism, public relations, broadcasting, content creation, and digital media. They're telling stories across multiple platforms and reaching audiences worldwide.

Business & Leadership

The leadership, teamwork, and project management skills developed through theatre productions have proven invaluable in business. Alumni hold leadership positions in corporations, run successful businesses, and excel in diverse professional fields.

Education & Arts Administration

Some alumni have become educators, teaching drama and theatre arts, inspiring the next generation of performers and storytellers. Others work in arts administration, managing cultural institutions and supporting the arts sector.

Diverse Professional Fields

DRAMSOC alumni succeed in law, medicine, engineering, finance, diplomacy, and more. The confidence, public speaking skills, emotional intelligence, and creative problem-solving developed through drama are assets in any profession.
